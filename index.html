Why Open Horizons

Not a repeat of school.
A structured alternative.

Most tutoring is reactive — a student struggles, a tutor is found, homework gets reviewed, the cycle continues. Open Horizons operates differently from the first session. Before any teaching begins, a structured diagnostic assessment identifies the specific conceptual gaps driving the difficulty. Instruction then starts from the right place, not an assumed one.

This is the difference between addressing a symptom and resolving a cause. Students who have been struggling for months — sometimes years — often find that a relatively short, targeted programme produces the kind of progress that general tutoring never delivered.

01
Diagnostic Precision
Every programme begins with structured assessment — finding the root of the difficulty, not just its surface symptoms.
02
Conceptual Clarity
Understanding before procedure. Reasoning before memorisation. Sessions rebuild from the foundation — not around it.

Personalised Growth
Individual learning goals, structured parent updates, and metacognitive strategies that extend progress beyond the sessions.

Evidence-Based Practice

Teaching grounded
in research.

Three established bodies of educational research — not buzzwords, but the frameworks that directly shape how every session is designed and delivered.

Read the full detail
01
Rosenshine's Principles of Instruction
Barak Rosenshine

Ten evidence-backed principles describing how expert teachers introduce content, check for understanding, and build student fluency.

02
Cognitive Load Theory
John Sweller

A theory of how working memory processes new information — informing how content is broken down, sequenced, and explained in every session.

03
Hattie's Visible Learning
John Hattie

A synthesis of 800+ meta-analyses identifying the highest-impact teaching strategies — separating what works from what is merely popular.

About the Tutor

A decade in classrooms.
Now working one-to-one.

Open Horizons was founded by Grant Crossman — a UAE-licensed tutor with over ten years of classroom teaching experience across British, American, and IB environments.
